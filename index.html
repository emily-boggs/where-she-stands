Where Women Drop Off

Representation at each level of the career ladder. Select an industry.

What you're seeing

Each bar shows the percentage of women at that career level. The bars get shorter as you climb — that's the "leaky pipeline."

The white bar marks the steepest single drop — where the most women are lost between levels.

Did you know?

For every 100 men promoted to manager, only 87 women are promoted. For women of color, that number drops to 73.

Does High Pay Mean Fast Growth?

Not always. Some industries pay well but promote slowly — and vice versa.

How to read this

X-axis (horizontal): How much women earn per dollar men earn. Further right = more equitable pay.

Y-axis (vertical): Years to first promotion. Lower on the chart = faster advancement.

The ideal is bottom-right: high pay and fast growth. No industry is there yet.

The trade-off

Public Sector pays the most fairly ($0.92) but promotes the slowest (8.5 yrs). Tech promotes fast but pays women 17 cents less per dollar.

Sources & Methodology

Figures are illustrative estimates informed by patterns from the following public reports:

  • U.S. Census Bureau, Current Population Survey — earnings by race and gender
  • McKinsey & LeanIn.org, Women in the Workplace (2023) — leadership pipeline data
  • Bureau of Labor Statistics — industry-level compensation and leave benchmarks
  • Catalyst.org — representation of women of color in corporate leadership
  • National Partnership for Women & Families — paid leave policy data

No live datasets were used. All values are approximations meant to reflect documented trends, not exact statistics.
