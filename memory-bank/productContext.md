# Product Context — Where She Stands

## Why This Exists
Data about gender inequity is widely available but rarely
presented in a way that is personal, visual, and actionable.
Most reports flatten the data into averages that hide the
experiences of women of color. This project exists to change
that — to make the data feel like it's about real people,
not statistics.

## What It Does
- Compares 6 industries across 4 equity dimensions
- Allows users to weight what matters most to them
- Reveals how gaps compound for women of color
- Delivers a personalized industry recommendation
- Tells the story through data, personas, and visual design

## What It Does NOT Do
- Does not use real/live data (all data is invented
  for storytelling purposes)
- Does not collect user data or require login
- Does not prescribe a single "right" answer —
  it surfaces trade-offs

## Industries Covered
1. Technology
2. Financial Services
3. Healthcare
4. Retail & Consumer Goods
5. Public Sector / Government
6. Manufacturing

## Dimensions Measured
1. 💰 Pay Equity
2. 📈 Leadership Representation
3. 👶 Family Leave Culture
4. 🚀 Career Growth

## Key Differentiator
The race/ethnicity filter layer — revealing that the
"average" hides who is really being left behind.

## Success Looks Like
A user lands on the page, engages with the personalizer,
filters by their identity, and walks away with a clear
understanding of which industries are truly best for women
— and which ones have the most work to do.
