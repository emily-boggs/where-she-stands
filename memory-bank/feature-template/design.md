# Design Direction — Where She Stands

## Concept
**"Art Deco Reckoning"**
The elegance and geometry of the 1920s meets the stark
clarity of modern data journalism. The aesthetic evokes
an era when women were fighting for their place in public
life — and reminds us that fight isn't over.

## Visual References
1. **Lemoine Mineral Water** — layered badge compositions,
   stylized female figure, geometric floral borders,
   ornate serif typography
2. **City Hall Steak & Cocktails Poster** — dark background,
   woman as compositional anchor, high-contrast type
3. **Purple Rain Cocktail Illustration** — validates the
   lavender/violet palette, jewel-tone confidence
4. **Eclipse of the Unknown / True Nature** — intricate
   black and white linework, layered geometric complexity
   on dark backgrounds

## Decorative Elements
- Geometric borders: chevrons, fan shapes, diamond motifs
- Intricate SVG linework dividers between sections
- Radial / sunburst accents as background texture
- Art Deco female figure silhouette as hero visual
- Badge / medallion compositions for "Best For" awards
- Sharp geometric chart edges — no rounded corners

## Mood
- Dark and weighty — serious data deserves serious design
- Purple as power — not pink, never pink
- Contrast is the message — gaps should feel stark
- Editorial, not corporate — magazine feature energy
- Ornate but intentional — every flourish earns its place

## What to Avoid
- ❌ Pink
- ❌ Soft gradients or rounded UI
- ❌ Clipart icons
- ❌ Light mode
- ❌ Dashboard / BI aesthetic
- ❌ Simple borders as the only decoration
