# Context — Where She Stands

## Project Background
Created as part of Slalom's P302 Interactive Data Story
challenge. The brief asked designers to pick an industry,
find something interesting in the data, and help someone
understand something they didn't before.

## Connection to Slalom Women
This project was conceived by a member of Slalom Women,
a community dedicated to advancing women at Slalom and
beyond. The story is personal — it reflects real questions
women ask when navigating career decisions.

## Framing Decision
Rather than pick a single industry, this project takes a
cross-industry lens — positioning itself as a guide for
women evaluating where to invest their professional energy.

## Data Philosophy
All data is invented and simplified for storytelling
purposes. It is inspired by and directionally consistent
with real research from:
- McKinsey Women in the Workplace Report
- Pew Research Center gender wage gap studies
- U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics

The goal is the story and how it's told — not the
precision of the dataset behind it.

## Intersectionality
A core design decision was to include a race/ethnicity
filter layer. The "overall" numbers for women mask
significant disparities for Black, Hispanic, and Asian
women. This project names that explicitly.

## Naming
The title "Where She Stands" was chosen because it:
- Works literally (here's where women stand in each industry)
- Works metaphorically (a stance, a reckoning, a position
  of power)
- Pairs with the Art Deco standing female figure
  as the hero visual
